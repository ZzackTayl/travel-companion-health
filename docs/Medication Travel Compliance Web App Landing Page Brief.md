## Page Goal & Audience

### Conversion Goal

Drive visitors to join the waitlist for early access to a mobile-first medication travel compliance web app. Secondary goal: validate demand by capturing the user’s top travel medication concern after signup.

### Target Visitor

* Traffic source: Organic search, travel health articles, disability/chronic illness communities, Reddit/forum referrals, clinician/travel clinic referrals, and early paid search around “traveling internationally with prescription medication.”
* Awareness level: They likely know traveling with medication can be risky or confusing, but they do not know which rules apply to their specific route, layovers, or medicine types.
* Key question: “Can I bring my medicine on this trip without getting delayed, questioned, or in trouble?”
* Primary objection: They may worry the product is giving generic or unreliable advice, especially because medication rules can have legal consequences.

This page should make the product feel trustworthy, cautious, and useful without overpromising legal or medical certainty. The strongest narrative is: official-source guidance first, route-specific preparation, accessible screenshot card, and anecdotal traveler context kept clearly separate from verified rules.

## Design Direction

### Visual Style

* Color palette: Deep navy background (#0B1220), soft medical blue primary (#2F80ED), mint/teal accent (#2DD4BF), warm caution amber (#F59E0B), clean off-white surfaces (#F8FAFC), and slate text (#334155).
* Typography: Highly legible sans-serif such as Inter, Source Sans 3, or IBM Plex Sans. Use large mobile-first type sizes and clear hierarchy.
* Spacing: Spacious enough to feel calm and trustworthy, but information-rich in the product mockups to demonstrate utility.
* Vibe: Trustworthy travel-health utility: closer to a government-grade health tool with polished consumer UX than a playful travel app.

### References

List 1-3 existing sites whose visual style to emulate and what specifically to borrow from each.

* Wise: Borrow the clear conversion flow, practical trust-building, and calm financial-risk communication.
* Clear or Global Entry-style travel pages: Borrow the airport/travel credibility cues and direct CTA structure, not the heavy government visual design.
* Apple Health / Health app product pages: Borrow the accessible card-based presentation, restrained color, and privacy-forward messaging.

### Brand Assets

* Logo, brand fonts, brand colors that must be used: None required yet. Recommend creating a simple wordmark and icon using a route line, cross/checkmark, or passport-card motif.
* Existing design system or component library: None required yet. Use accessible components with visible focus states, large touch targets, semantic forms, and high-contrast status indicators.

### Responsive

* Mobile-first or desktop-first: Mobile-first.
* Key mobile adaptations: Single-column layout, sticky bottom CTA after scroll, stacked proof/feature cards, collapsible source explanation, phone mockup sized to fit without horizontal scrolling.
* Animation and interaction preferences: Subtle only. Use gentle route-line animation, card reveal on scroll, and reduced-motion support. Avoid distracting motion because the audience may be anxious or using assistive technology.
